# Vision

## Mission

Create the world's most intuitive and trusted spatial property inspection platform.

LUZ Spatial Lab explores how spatial computing, AI and structured property data can transform recurring property inspections from isolated reports into a living digital memory of every property.

---

# The Problem

Traditional property inspections create documents.

Those documents are valuable for a short period but become increasingly difficult to use over time.

Owners and inspectors often struggle to answer questions such as:

- Has this crack grown?
- When did this moisture first appear?
- How many times has the pool pump been repaired?
- Which contractor worked on this issue?
- Is this a recurring problem?

Although the information exists, it is scattered across reports, photos, emails and invoices.

---

# The Vision

Every property should have a continuously evolving digital history.

Instead of browsing folders and PDFs, an owner should be able to explore the property naturally.

Selecting a room or asset should reveal:

- previous inspections;
- historical photos;
- findings over time;
- contractor visits;
- maintenance records;
- completed repairs;
- recurring issues;
- documents and warranties.

The property becomes a structured source of knowledge rather than a collection of disconnected files.

---

# The Property Passport

Every property begins with a one-time Property Passport.

The Property Passport contains:

- simplified spatial layout;
- room structure;
- technical assets;
- inspection checkpoints;
- baseline photographs;
- important documentation;
- service information;
- property metadata.

It becomes the permanent reference model for future inspections.

---

# Living Property History

Every inspection adds another layer to the property's history.

Nothing is overwritten.

Instead, observations accumulate over time.

Example:

Kitchen Sink

2019  
✓ Good condition

2021  
⚠ Minor leak detected

2022  
🔧 Seal replaced

2024  
⚠ Humidity below cabinet

2025  
✓ Dry after repair

The complete history remains available.

---

# The Inspector Experience

Technology should disappear into the background.

The inspector should focus on observing the property rather than operating software.

The ideal inspection feels like:

- walk naturally;
- inspect professionally;
- speak observations;
- capture evidence;
- continue.

The system quietly organises everything.

---

# The Owner Experience

Owners should always understand the condition of their property.

Instead of receiving isolated inspection reports, they receive an evolving picture of their investment.

They should immediately understand:

- overall health;
- changes since the last inspection;
- open issues;
- completed maintenance;
- upcoming recommendations.

Trust is built through transparency and continuity.

---

# Artificial Intelligence

AI is an assistant.

Its responsibilities include:

- transcribing voice;
- extracting structured findings;
- identifying similar previous issues;
- drafting reports;
- highlighting inconsistencies;
- recommending follow-up actions.

AI should never replace professional judgement.

The inspector always approves the final inspection.

---

# Spatial Computing

Spatial technology exists to improve navigation and context.

It should help answer questions such as:

- Which room am I in?
- Which checkpoint is nearby?
- Where was this finding recorded?
- What has changed since last year?

The spatial model is a navigation layer, not the final product.

---

# Guiding Principles

Every feature should support at least one of these goals:

- reduce inspection effort;
- improve inspection quality;
- preserve property knowledge;
- simplify owner communication;
- enable long-term property history.

If a feature does not contribute meaningfully to one of these objectives, it should not become part of the platform.

---

# Success

Success is not measured by the sophistication of the technology.

Success is measured by whether inspectors complete better inspections with less effort, and whether owners gain greater confidence in the care and condition of their property.

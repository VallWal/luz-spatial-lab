# Roadmap

## Philosophy

LUZ Spatial Lab is a research project.

The objective is not to build features as quickly as possible.

The objective is to validate ideas before they become part of the production LUZ Inspector platform.

Every phase should answer one important question.

Only validated concepts move forward.

---

# Phase 1 — Spatial Foundation

## Objective

Can we reliably create and reuse a lightweight digital representation of a property?

## Deliverables

- RoomPlan integration
- Single-room scanning
- Save spatial model
- Reload spatial model
- Room naming
- Property structure
- Model versioning
- Basic floor plan

## Success Criteria

- Scan completes in less than 5 minutes
- Model reloads consistently
- Room names persist
- Inspector understands the floor plan immediately

---

# Phase 2 — Checkpoints

## Objective

Can inspections become location-aware?

## Deliverables

- Manual checkpoint placement
- Checkpoint editing
- Room association
- Inspection items linked to checkpoints
- Required evidence rules
- QR/NFC support research

## Success Criteria

- Checkpoints remain stable after reload
- Inspectors can navigate naturally
- Less manual searching than today's workflow

---

# Phase 3 — Spatial Inspections

## Objective

Can recurring inspections use the Property Passport effectively?

## Deliverables

- Load Property Passport
- Current room detection
- Room progress
- Checkpoint completion
- Finding placement
- Spatial anchors
- Inspection summary

## Success Criteria

- Inspector completes an inspection faster than with a checklist alone
- Room context reduces missed inspection items
- Findings are correctly associated with rooms and checkpoints

---

# Phase 4 — Property History

## Objective

Can every inspection contribute to a meaningful long-term property record?

## Deliverables

- Finding timeline
- Observation history
- Maintenance events
- Contractor history
- Inspection comparisons
- Room history

## Success Criteria

- Owners can understand the evolution of a property over time
- Historical information is easy to access
- No previous inspection data is lost

---

# Phase 5 — AI Assistant

## Objective

Can AI reduce administrative work without replacing inspector judgement?

## Deliverables

- Speech transcription
- Structured extraction
- Duplicate detection
- Suggested report text
- Comparison with previous findings
- Confidence scoring

## Success Criteria

- AI saves measurable inspection time
- Inspector corrections remain minimal
- False assumptions are rare
- Inspector always approves the final result

---

# Phase 6 — Production Integration

## Objective

Can validated components be integrated into the existing LUZ Inspector application?

## Deliverables

- Integration strategy
- Shared data contracts
- Backend interfaces
- Synchronisation with Airtable
- n8n workflow compatibility
- Migration plan

## Success Criteria

- Existing production workflows continue to operate
- No disruption for current customers
- Spatial functionality can be enabled incrementally

---

# Long-Term Vision

Future research may include:

- Exterior mapping
- Drone-assisted inspections
- Matterport compatibility
- Digital twins
- Predictive maintenance
- Automatic change detection
- Mixed reality guidance
- Indoor navigation
- Contractor assistance
- Owner self-service inspections

These topics are intentionally outside the initial scope.

---

# Guiding Rule

The production LUZ Inspector application always takes priority.

No experimental feature should delay, complicate or destabilise the production product.

LUZ Spatial Lab exists to explore the future, while LUZ Inspector delivers value today.
